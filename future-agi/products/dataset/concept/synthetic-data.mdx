## Title: Synthetic Data

---

Synthetic Data is a type of data that is artificially augmented instead of scarped off from real-world events. It is generated using set of rules, patterns, algorithms and models to replicate the properties of real world data without hampering privacy or copyright law. They are used to refine your models and distill them for various specific use cases and workflows.

Synthetic data is very intelligent in design and it reflects the real world distributions, edge cases or constraints without bringing in the noise that is often found in real world data. Therefore they are very powerful in the field of machine learning , testing and product development workflows.

---

## Key Characteristics:

- Schema-Driven: You are asked to define the columns, types and the description for the datapoints you want to generate
- Realistic Distribution: The data that is generated follows rules, patterns and are maintaining a distribution, they aren’t random values
- Safety: Data can be completely harmless such that it doesn’t have personally identifiable information, or toxicity etc.

---

## When to use Synthetic Data

- When real data is unavailable, incomplete, or too sensitive to use
- When testing systems at scale or simulating rare scenarios
- When training AI models that require balanced or diverse inputs
